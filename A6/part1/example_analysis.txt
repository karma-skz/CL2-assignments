2. Concrete Objects/Physical Context
   - Physical objects as direct arguments (e.g., "build centres")
   - Literal usage over metaphorical (e.g., "build" succeeded, "see" failed)

3. Domain-Specific Vocabulary
   - Clustering of specialized terms helps (e.g., legal context for "serve")
   - Limited by gloss coverage of domain terms

4. WordNet Example Sentences
   - Examples provide contextual usage patterns
   - Often more helpful than abstract definitions

5. Collocation Patterns
   - Fixed expressions like "keep under review" aid disambiguation
   - Algorithm fails when idioms are not recognized as units
